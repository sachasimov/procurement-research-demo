### 1. Executive Summary
As of mid-2026, importing unwrought aluminium and aluminium packaging products (sheet/foil) into the United States and the European Union requires navigating highly volatile, protectionist trade regimes. The US landscape is dominated by a June 2025 increase in Section 232 tariffs to 50% for most nations, an April 2026 restructuring that applies these duties to the full customs value of goods, and a complete embargo on Russian metal. The EU landscape is defined by the January 1, 2026, implementation of the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) definitive phase, which imposes significant carbon-linked financial liabilities on importers, alongside aggressive anti-dumping duties on Chinese products and a phased total ban on Russian aluminium set to culminate in 2027.

---

### 2. United States: Tariff and Trade Landscape

#### 2.1. Base Tariffs (MFN / Column 1 Rates)
Standard import duties for aluminium products are classified under the Harmonized Tariff Schedule (HTS) Chapter 76. Base rates remain relatively low, though they are largely superseded by Section 232 tariffs.
*   **Unwrought Aluminium (HTS 7601):** 
    *   Non-alloyed (HTS 7601.10.30, least dimension ≤9.5mm): 2.6% [HTS CHAPTER 76 ALUMINUM AND ARTICLES THEREOF](https://www.usitc.gov/publications/docs/tata/hts/bychapter/1000c76.pdf).
    *   Other non-alloyed (HTS 7601.10.60): Free [HTS CHAPTER 76 ALUMINUM AND ARTICLES THEREOF](https://www.usitc.gov/publications/docs/tata/hts/bychapter/1000c76.pdf).
    *   Alloys (HTS 7601.20.30, least dimension ≤9.5mm): 2.6%; Alloys with ≥25% silicon (HTS 7601.20.60): 2.1%; Other alloys (HTS 7601.20.90): Free [HTS CHAPTER 76 ALUMINUM AND ARTICLES THEREOF](https://www.usitc.gov/publications/docs/tata/hts/bychapter/1000c76.pdf).
*   **Aluminium Sheet/Plates (HTS 7606):** Non-alloyed (7606.11.30) and alloyed (7606.12.30) rectangular plates/sheets over 0.2 mm thick are subject to a 3% duty [HTS CHAPTER 76 ALUMINUM AND ARTICLES THEREOF](https://www.usitc.gov/publications/docs/tata/hts/bychapter/1000c76.pdf).
*   **Aluminium Foil (HTS 7607):** 
    *   Thickness ≤0.01 mm (7607.11.30): 5.8% [HTS CHAPTER 76 ALUMINUM AND ARTICLES THEREOF](https://www.usitc.gov/publications/docs/tata/hts/bychapter/1000c76.pdf).
    *   Thickness 0.01 mm–0.15 mm (7607.11.60): 5.3% [HTS CHAPTER 76 ALUMINUM AND ARTICLES THEREOF](https://www.usitc.gov/publications/docs/tata/hts/bychapter/1000c76.pdf).
    *   Thickness 0.15 mm–0.2 mm (7607.11.90): 3% [HTS CHAPTER 76 ALUMINUM AND ARTICLES THEREOF](https://www.usitc.gov/publications/docs/tata/hts/bychapter/1000c76.pdf).
    *   *Note:* A 5.9% general rate increase is scheduled to take effect on January 1, 2026, specifically for HTS codes 7607.11.60 and 7607.11.90 [HTS Code 7607.11.60 US import tariffs and quota - UNIS](https://www.unisco.com/hts/76071160).

#### 2.2. Section 232 Tariffs & 2025-2026 Restructuring
The US has aggressively expanded its Section 232 national security tariffs on aluminium:
*   **Rate Increases:** Effective June 4, 2025, Section 232 tariffs on aluminium and derivatives were increased from 25% to 50% ad valorem for most countries [Section 232 Tariffs on Steel and Aluminum – Executive Order on Increase of Tariff Rate | AWPA](https://www.awpa.org/2025/06/04/section-232-tariffs-on-steel-and-aluminum-executive-order-on-increase-of-tariff-rate/). The UK is a notable exception, retaining a 25% rate [Section 232 Update: 50% Tariff on Aluminum Imports – New Reporting and Classification Rules | Buckland Customs](https://www.buckland.com/news/section-232-update-50-tariff-on-aluminum-imports-new-reporting-and-classification-rules/).
*   **Customs Value Rule:** Effective April 6, 2026, Section 232 duties apply to the *full customs value* of the imported product, rather than just the metal content value [Federal Register :: Strengthening Actions Taken To Adjust Imports of Aluminum, Steel, and Copper Into the United States](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/09/2026-06960/strengthening-actions-taken-to-adjust-imports-of-aluminum-steel-and-copper-into-the-united-states).
*   **Tiered Rates & US-Metal Thresholds:** An April 2, 2026, Presidential Proclamation structured duties into tiers: 50% for primary metal, 25% for certain derivatives, and a temporary 15% rate for specific industrial equipment through 2027 [Section 232 Tariffs on Steel and Aluminum | Congress.gov | Library of Congress](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IN12519). Furthermore, as of June 8, 2026, derivative products qualify for a reduced 10% tariff if they are made of at least 85% US-sourced metal by weight (reduced from a previous 95% threshold) [U.S. Adjusts Section 232 Tariffs on Aluminum, Steel and Copper](https://www.ghy.com/trade-compliance/us-adjusts-section-232-tariffs-on-aluminum-steel-and-copper-full-customs-value-now-applies/).
*   **Unknown Origin:** If the country of smelt/cast is reported as "unknown," a punitive 200% tariff is assessed [Section 232 Steel and aluminum import tariff | International Trade](https://www.craneww.com/knowledge-center/trade-advisory-notices/section-232-steel-and-aluminum-import-tariff/).

#### 2.3. The Section 122 Global Surcharge (Contested)
On February 24, 2026, the US implemented a temporary 10% global import surcharge under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 [Trump Administration Imposes 10% Section 122 Tariff in Plan to Replace IEEPA Tariffs | White & Case LLP](https://www.whitecase.com/insight-alert/trump-administration-imposes-10-section-122-tariff-plan-replace-ieepa-tariffs). 
*   **Interaction with Aluminium:** Section 122 does *not* stack with Section 232; aluminium products subject to Section 232 are exempt from the 122 surcharge [Section 122 Tariff 2026: Rate, Exemptions & What Changed | Triangle Trade Intelligence](https://triangle-trade-intel.site/blog/section-122-tariff-what-importers-need-to-know). However, hundreds of low-metal-content derivatives removed from Section 232 coverage in April 2026 subsequently fell under the 10% Section 122 tariff [United States modifies steel, aluminum, and copper Section 232 tariffs | White & Case LLP](https://www.whitecase.com/insight-alert/united-states-modifies-steel-aluminum-and-copper-section-232-tariffs).
*   **Legal Status:** On May 7, 2026, the US Court of International Trade ruled the Section 122 tariffs unlawful. However, on May 12, 2026, an appellate court issued a stay, allowing Customs to continue collecting the 10% surcharge from all importers (except the specific plaintiffs) while the appeal proceeds [Section 122 Stay: Why Importers Still Pay the 10% | Falcone Global](https://falconeglobal.com/news/section-122-tariffs-court-ruling-appeal-and-stay/).

#### 2.4. Country-Specific Sanctions and AD/CVDs
*   **Russia:** The US maintains a 200% ad valorem tariff on Russian aluminium and derivatives, including products where any primary aluminium was smelted/cast in Russia [A Proclamation on Adjusting Imports of Aluminum Into the United States | The White House](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefing-room/presidential-actions/2023/02/24/a-proclamation-on-adjusting-imports-of-aluminum-into-the-united-states-4/). Furthermore, the Treasury Department outright prohibited the importation of Russian-origin aluminium produced on or after April 13, 2024 [United States and United Kingdom Take Action to Reduce Russian Revenue from Metals | U.S. Department of the Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/jy2249).
*   **USMCA (Canada/Mexico):** For qualifying USMCA derivatives, the 25% Section 232 duty applies only to the *non-U.S. content* value, provided the total effective duty is at least 15% [FURTHER ADJUSTING THE TARIFF REGIMES FOR IMPORTS OF ALUMINUM, STEEL, AND COPPER INTO THE UNITED STATES 11032](https://public-inspection.federalregister.gov/2026-11314.pdf).
*   **China:** While the US and China agreed in November 2025 to reduce certain bilateral tariffs from 125% to 10%, this reciprocal reduction *does not apply* to aluminium, which remains under Section 232 [China Tariff Update: November 4 Executive Orders Signal Temporary Easing, Not Policy Reversal - Law Offices of Snell & Wilmer](https://www.swlaw.com/publication/china-tariff-update-november-4-executive-orders-signal-temporary-easing-not-policy-reversal/). Additionally, Chinese aluminium foil faces historical AD/CVDs (AD up to 151.04%, CVD up to 115.73%) [Anti-dumping and countervailing duties on aluminum foil exported from China - Knowledge](https://www.cnaluminiumfoil.com/info/anti-dumping-and-countervailing-duties-on-alum-79869239.html), and new AD/CVD orders were issued in 2025 for Chinese disposable aluminium containers [U.S. to Impose AD/CVD on Chinese Aluminum Containers | CH Powell Company](https://www.chpowell.com/newsroom/us-to-impose-ad-cvd-on-chinese-aluminum-containers/).

### 3. European Union: Tariff and Trade Landscape

#### 3.1. Base Tariffs (TARIC Codes)
The EU utilizes a 10-digit TARIC coding system based on the 2026 Combined Nomenclature (CN) [Commission publishes the 2026 version of the Combined Nomenclature - Taxation and Customs Union](https://taxation-customs.ec.europa.eu/news/commission-publishes-2026-version-combined-nomenclature-2025-10-31_en). 
*   **Unwrought Aluminium (7601):** Baseline import duties range from 3% for unalloyed aluminium to 6% for aluminium alloys [Column: Europe's aluminium industry has its own tariff problems | Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/article/idUSKCN1TM1JU/). Unwrought aluminium is subject to specific quotas [HS Code for EU Aluminum with Exclusion | FreightAmigo](https://www.freightamigo.com/en/blog/hs-code/hs-code-for-eu-aluminum-with-exclusion-1754276404428/).
*   **Aluminium Sheet (7606):** Covers plates/sheets >0.2 mm thick (e.g., TARIC 7606 11 91 25) [COMMISSION IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) 2021/582](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=CELEX:32021R0582).
*   **Aluminium Foil (7607):** Covers foil ≤0.2 mm thick (e.g., TARIC 7607 19 10 90) [7607 1910 90 - Aluminium foil](https://www.taricsupport.com/nomenclature/en/7607191090.html).

#### 3.2. Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) - Definitive Phase
The most significant change to EU aluminium imports is the definitive phase of CBAM, which launched on January 1, 2026, shifting from reporting-only to mandatory financial obligations [CBAM Definitive Phase 2026 Malaysian Manufacturers Exporting to EU Must Prepare Now - Bernard Business Consulting](https://bernardbc.com/cbam-definitive-phase-2026-malaysian-manufacturers-exporting-to-eu-must-prepare-now/).
*   **Compliance:** Importers of 7601, 7606, and 7607 products must be "authorised CBAM declarants" [EU’s Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism Enters Definitive Phase to Curb Carbon Leakage – ESG BROADCAST](https://esgbroadcast.com/broadcast/eus-carbon-border-adjustment-mechanism-enters-definitive-phase-to-curb-carbon-leakage/). A de minimis exemption applies for importers bringing in less than 50 tonnes annually [CBAM Explained 2026 | Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (EU) 2023/956 | GreenSutra®](https://greensutra.in/news/cbam-explained-2026/).
*   **Financial Liability:** Importers must purchase CBAM certificates linked to the EU ETS auction price (reported at €75.36 per tonne of CO2e in April 2026) [AI Blog for Governments and Enterprises | Net0](https://net0.com/blog/the-eu-carbon-border-adjustment-mechanism-cbam). In 2026, liability is moderated by a 97.5% free allocation deduction, which phases out to 0% by 2034 [CBAM - REYHER - Verbindungselemente und Befestigungstechnik](https://www.reyher.de/en/company/cbam/).
*   **Verification:** 2026 emissions data must be verified by an accredited third party, requiring on-site inspections during the first year [CBAM enters the definitive period: key regulatory updates and scope extension | carboneer](https://carboneer.earth/en/2025/12/cbam-definitive-period-scope-extension/). Non-compliance triggers penalties up to €100 per tonne of CO2e plus the cost of missing certificates [Comparing the UK and EU CBAM: Key Differences in Carbon Border Adjustment Policies - Redshaw Advisors](https://redshawadvisors.com/comparing-the-uk-and-eu-cbam-key-differences-in-carbon-border-adjustment-policies/).

#### 3.3. Sanctions and Safeguards
*   **Russia:** The EU is aggressively phasing out Russian aluminium. The 12th package (Dec 2023) banned wire, foil, and tubes [EU adopts 12th package of sanctions against Russia for its continued illegal war against Ukraine - Enlargement and Eastern Neighbourhood](https://enlargement.ec.europa.eu/news/eu-adopts-12th-package-sanctions-against-russia-its-continued-illegal-war-against-ukraine-2023-12-19_en). The 16th package (Feb 2025) banned unwrought aluminium (7601), allowing a transitional quota of 275,000 metric tons through February 26, 2026 [Questions and answers on the sixteenth package of restrictive measures against Russia - Enlargement and Eastern Neighbourhood](https://enlargement.ec.europa.eu/news/questions-and-answers-sixteenth-package-restrictive-measures-against-russia-2025-02-24_en). A secondary 50,000-ton quota applies to pre-existing contracts until December 31, 2026, after which a total ban takes effect in 2027 [Recent key sanctions imposed against Russia by the EU and the UK - Skuld](https://www.skuld.com/topics/legal/sanctions/russia/recent-key-sanctions-imposed-against-russia-by-the-eu-and-the-uk/).
*   **Norway & Iceland:** Despite their EEA status, the EU imposed new safeguards effective November 18, 2025. Imports are duty-free up to a quota of 75% of historical (2022-2024) volumes. Volumes exceeding this quota face a variable duty triggered if import prices fall below a specific price floor [Norway stung by new EU tariff | Norway's News in English](https://www.newsinenglish.no/2025/11/18/norway-stung-by-new-eu-tariff/).
*   **Mozambique:** Exempt from baseline duties under the Everything But Arms (EBA) initiative, but fully subject to CBAM carbon taxes starting in 2026 [EU carbon tax prompts "green aluminium" claims in Mozambique](https://www.climatechangenews.com/2023/07/03/mozambique-aluminium-cbam-carbon-border-tax/).

#### 3.4. Anti-Dumping and Countervailing Duties (AD/CVD)
The EU heavily targets Chinese aluminium imports with trade defense instruments:
*   **Flat-Rolled Products (7606/7607):** Definitive AD duties are set at a standard rate of 24.6% (individual company rates range from 14.3% to 21.4%). These are scheduled to expire on October 12, 2026, though a partial interim review was initiated in March 2026 [Trade and Industry Department - EU: Impending expiry of the anti-dumping measures on imports of aluminium flat-rolled products originating in Chinese Mainland](https://www.tid.gov.hk/en/tradecircular/2026/ci962026.html).
*   **Converter Foil (ex 7607 11 19):** AD/CVD rates range from 15.4% to 28.5%, expected to expire December 9, 2026 [EU duties on Chinese aluminium converter foil to expire in 2026](https://www.alcircle.com/news/eu-duties-on-chinese-aluminium-converter-foil-to-expire-in-2026-117829).

### 4. Supply Chain Impacts and Risk Assessment
The compounding effects of US tariffs and EU carbon taxes are fundamentally restructuring global aluminium flows. 
*   **Cost Premiums:** US buyers are currently paying a 68% premium over the London Metal Exchange (LME) price for physical metal as of January 2026 due to the combined impact of 50% Section 232 tariffs and global supply shifts [US aluminium consumers pay the spiralling cost of tariffs | Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/markets/commodities/metals/us-aluminium-consumers-pay-spiralling-cost-tariffs-2026-01-21/).
*   **Trade Diversion:** Producers are redirecting export volumes away from North America toward the EU to capture higher margins, while Canadian and Mexican processors are heavily leveraging USMCA trade benefits to supply the US market [Global market faces tariff impact, reveals aluminium supply chain vulnerabilities](https://www.alcircle.com/news/market-faces-tariff-impact-reveals-aluminium-supply-chain-vulnerabilities-117266).
*   **Compliance Overhead:** EU importers face severe administrative burdens in 2026. Total CBAM costs for EU importers are estimated to exceed €12 billion in 2026 (roughly 15% of covered import value) [Provisional CBAM benchmarks announced: how will this restructure global trade? - Fastmarkets](https://www.fastmarkets.com/insights/provisional-cbam-benchmarks-announced-how-will-this-restructure-global-trade/). Sourcing from high-emitting countries without primary data forces the use of EU default values, which carry mark-ups of up to 30% [CBAM enters the definitive period: key regulatory updates and scope extension | carboneer](https://carboneer.earth/en/2025/12/cbam-definitive-period-scope-extension/).

### 5. Gaps and Limitations
*   **Exact EU MFN Rates for 7606/7607:** While the baseline MFN duty for unwrought aluminium (7601) is confirmed at 3-6%, the exact standard MFN percentage rates for sheet (7606) and foil (7607) are not explicitly detailed in the available findings, beyond the specific AD/CVD rates applied to China.
*   **US AD/CVD Current Rates:** The findings provide historical ranges for US AD/CVDs on Chinese foil (up to 151% AD / 115% CVD) and note new 2025 orders for containers, but do not provide the exact finalized 2026 AD/CVD percentages for these specific HTS codes.
*   **Section 122 Stacking Contradiction:** There is minor conflict in the trade press regarding whether the US Section 122 surcharge can ever stack with Section 232; while primary consensus indicates they do not stack, some consulting sources suggest potential combined exposure for certain edge-case products.
