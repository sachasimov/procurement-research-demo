## Query sent

> Summarise the regulatory, food-contact, and labelling requirements for importing and selling packaged beer in glass bottles into the European Union market as of 2026.
>
> Cover:
> - Mandatory on-label information for beer sold in the EU (e.g. alcoholic strength, net quantity, ingredients/allergens, energy/nutrition, responsible operator, lot/batch)
> - Language requirements
> - Food-contact material rules relevant to labels, inks and adhesives touching the product
> - Packaging and packaging-waste obligations, including deposit-return scheme trends and extended producer responsibility
> - Any recent or upcoming changes (e.g. EU Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation)
>
> Where possible, cite official sources (EUR-Lex, European Commission, national authorities). Note where requirements differ by member state, and flag anything that is still proposed rather than in force.

## Answer

**Executive Summary**

As of July 2026, importing and selling packaged beer in glass bottles into the European Union requires compliance with a dual framework: the established Food Information to Consumers (FIC) Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 and the newly enforceable Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR) (EU) 2025/40. While beer above 1.2% ABV remains exempt from EU-wide mandatory ingredient and nutrition labeling, national exceptions (such as Germany's purity laws) and voluntary industry standards heavily influence label design. Crucially, the PPWR—which takes full effect on August 12, 2026—introduces strict Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) mandates, requiring non-EU importers to appoint an Authorized Representative (AR) and print their contact details physically on the label. Furthermore, new chemical restrictions ban BPA in adhesives and inks by July 20, 2026, and PFAS in food-contact packaging by August 12, 2026.

---

**1. Mandatory On-Label Information**

Under the FIC Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011, beer labels must meet specific formatting and informational requirements. Mandatory food information must be printed in a font size with an x-height of at least 1.2 mm, or 0.9 mm for packages with a surface area smaller than 80 cm² [Food Labelling: All Rules and Mandatory Information Under EU 1169/2011]. Stickers are permitted for mandatory labeling provided they are not easily removable [EU-28 Food and Agricultural Import Regulations and Standards Report].

*   **Alcoholic Strength and Net Quantity:** Beverages containing more than 1.2% alcohol by volume (ABV) must display their actual alcoholic strength by volume [Consolidated TEXT: 32011R1169 — EN — 01.04.2025]. The net quantity and the name/address of the food business operator are also mandatory [Alcohol labelling - BRIEFING EPRS | European Parliamentary Research Service].
*   **Ingredients and Nutrition:** Alcoholic beverages above 1.2% ABV are currently exempt from mandatory EU ingredient lists and nutrition declarations [Alcohol labelling - Food Safety - European Commission]. However, if a nutrition declaration is provided voluntarily, it may be limited to the energy value alone [Alcohol labelling - BRIEFING EPRS | European Parliamentary Research Service]. By 2022, voluntary initiatives led 95% of beer volumes sold in the EU to include an ingredient list and 88% to label energy values [Nutrition and ingredient labelling in the EU alcohol sector: the strange mix of mandatory and voluntary initiatives].
*   **Allergens:** The labeling of the 14 most common allergens (e.g., cereals containing gluten) is mandatory, even without a full ingredient list [Alcohol labelling: State of play - European Parliament]. 
*   **Durability and Lot/Batch Marking:** Beers below 10% ABV must indicate a date of minimum durability [Alcohol labelling: State of play - European Parliament]. Under Directive 2011/91/EU, all foodstuffs must bear a lot/batch identifier determined by the producer or first EU seller, preceded by the letter "L" (unless clearly distinguishable) [Directive 2011/91/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council of 13 December 2011 on indications or marks identifying the lot to which a foodstuff belongs (codification) (Text with EEA relevance)]. The lot number does not need to appear in the same field of vision as the product name or ABV [International Affairs Resources for Belgium | TTB: Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau].

**National Divergences in Labeling**
Article 41 of the FIC Regulation allows Member States to maintain national measures for alcoholic beverage ingredients [Alcohol labelling - BRIEFING EPRS | European Parliamentary Research Service]. 
*   **Germany:** Since 1994, under the *Vorläufiges Biergesetz* and *Bierverordnung*, beer marketed in Germany must be labeled with a list of ingredients in descending order of weight, preceded by the word "Zutaten" [BMLEH - Food labelling - EU-wide uniform food labelling lmiv-fic] [Germany | TTB: Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau]. Imported beers not conforming to the German *Reinheitsgebot* (Purity Law) must list all nonconforming ingredients [The Reinheitsgebot - One Country's Interpretation of Quality Beer – MoreBeer!].
*   **Ireland:** Ireland has postponed the implementation of mandatory cancer warnings on alcohol labels until September 2028 [Alcohol labelling: State of play - European Parliament].

**Language Requirements**
Article 15 of the FIC Regulation requires mandatory information to appear in a language easily understood by consumers where the food is marketed, though multi-language labels are permitted [Language and presentation of food information - Food Safety] [L_2011304EN.01001801.xml - EUR-Lex - European Union].

**2. Food Contact Materials (FCM): Glass, Inks, and Adhesives**

Glass, adhesives, and printing inks fall under the framework Regulation (EC) No 1935/2004 and the Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) Regulation (EC) No 2023/2006 [Food Contact Materials - Food Safety - European Commission] [Legislation - Food Safety - European Commission]. While glass is low-risk—research shows only 47 chemicals migrating from glass/ceramics compared to 1,975 from plastics—regulators are tightening rules [Food Safety and Compliance: Why Our Glass Packaging Meets the Highest Standards - Pattesons].

*   **BPA and PFAS Bans:** Commission Regulation (EU) 2024/3190 prohibits Bisphenol A (BPA) in food contact adhesives and printing inks, with single-use items required to comply by July 20, 2026 [EU Guidance on BPA and Other Bisphenols Ban in Food-Contact Materials]. Additionally, the PPWR bans per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) in food-contact packaging effective August 12, 2026 [Food contact materials in the EU: State of play].
*   **Heavy Metals:** The European Commission is lowering migration limits for lead and cadmium from vitreous materials like glass [Revision of EU rules on FCMs - Food Safety - European Union]. While health certificates are normally not required for beer imports, customs laboratories perform controls, and consignments can face border rejections for non-compliant metal migration [Importing drinks | Food Standards Agency] [EU Customs Laboratories - Taxation and Customs Union - European Commission] [Serious Notifications on Food Contact Materials in the EU RASFF - PMC].
*   **Inks and Adhesives:** There are no EU-wide harmonized specific measures for inks and adhesives [Food Contact Materials - Regulation (EC) 1935/2004 | Think Tank | European Parliament]. However, Germany has reintroduced a national Printing Ink Ordinance with a positive list of roughly 600 chemicals, phasing in until December 2026 [Printing inks and adhesive chemicals detected in a wide variety of foods | Food Packaging Forum].

**3. Packaging, Waste, and the 2026 PPWR**

The Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation (EU) 2025/40 (PPWR) entered into force on February 11, 2025, and applies in full from August 12, 2026 [PPWR Compliance Guide 2026: What Changes 12 August]. The regulation introduces a broad definition of "producer" that includes importers, making them responsible for Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) registration, reporting, and financing waste management [The new EU packaging regulation: Key requirements from August 2026 | Gleiss Lutz].

**Authorized Representative (AR) and Traceability**
*   **Mandatory Appointment:** Non-EU producers must appoint an Authorized Representative (AR) in each Member State where they sell goods to handle EPR duties [EPR Compliance in the EU: 2026 Guide | Complir]. Failure to do so by August 12, 2026, can result in sales bans and fines up to €200,000 [PPWR Requirements 2026 — EU Directive Packaging Law, EPR Registration and Full Compliance Guide - Lovat Compliance].
*   **Physical Labeling Requirement:** Starting August 12, 2026, packaging must physically bear the name, registered trade name or trademark, and a postal address for the manufacturer and the importer/AR [Get ready for the EU’s packaging regulation: PPWR Requirements that are effective from August]. The European Commission has confirmed under the overlapping General Product Safety Regulation (GPSR) that digital labels (e.g., QR codes) cannot replace physical labels for mandatory contact information [Labeling Requirements for GPSR Compliance - EARP].
*   **Declarations of Conformity:** Importers must ensure the manufacturer has drawn up technical documentation and an EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC), which the AR must retain for 5 years for single-use packaging and 10 years for reusable packaging [PPWR Declaration of Conformity: what it is, when you need it and how to get it from suppliers].

**Deposit-Return Schemes (DRS) and Reuse Targets**
The PPWR mandates that Member States establish a DRS for single-use plastic and metal beverage containers by January 1, 2029, but only requires them to "endeavour" to establish a DRS for glass [European Commission calls for mandatory Deposit and Return systems in Member States | DWF Group]. 
*   **Current Glass DRS:** Germany includes glass bottles in its DRS (deposits of €0.08 and €0.15) [Overview and results of Deposit Return Schemes in Europe]. Poland's DRS (operational in 2026) includes reusable glass bottles up to 1.5 liters (1 PLN deposit) [Which Countries Have Deposit Return Schemes in 2026? | TOMRA]. Belgium maintains a deposit on reusable glass beer bottles [Race towards Deposit Return Systems | Fair Resource Foundation].
*   **Reuse Targets:** By 2030, beverage distributors must ensure 10% of products are in reusable packaging [Packaging and packaging waste (from 2026) | EUR-Lex]. Reusable packaging must bear a specific label indicating reusability and, by February 2029, carry a QR code to facilitate tracking [The new EU packaging regulation: Key requirements from August 2026 | Gleiss Lutz] [PPWR Compliance 2026: Guide for Manufacturers & Importers].

**4. Environmental Labeling and the Triman Dispute**

The PPWR introduces a single harmonized EU labeling system for sortability and material markings, which will replace national schemes by August 12, 2028 [PPWR labelling requirements explained: sortability, material and reuse markings]. 

Until then, national rules apply, most notably France's Triman logo. The European Commission has referred France to the Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU), arguing the Triman logo restricts trade [European Commission Takes France to Court Over Triman Logo]. Despite this, France intends to enforce the Triman logo until the 2028 PPWR harmonization, with fines up to €15,000 per violation [Triman Logo Dispute – French EPR Labelling vs. EU Harmonisation] [The Complete 2026 Guide to Triman Labeling and EPR Compliance in France]. 

*Crucial Exemption for Glass:* Household glass beverage containers are specifically exempted from the mandatory Triman logo and Info-Tri marking requirements under the French AGEC Law [European Commission Takes France to the CJEU Over Mandatory Triman Signage | ChemLinked]. 

---

**5. Industry Impact and Criticisms**

The Brewers of Europe have heavily criticized the PPWR for creating uncertainty. Key industry concerns include:
*   **Asset Stranding:** A lack of clarity on reuse targets risks the premature withdrawal of reusable assets like crates, weakening existing DRS systems [Brewers of Europe call for urgent clarification on EU packaging rules following Commission communication | The Brewers of Europe]. Brewers also require clarification on whether B2B reusable kegs count toward reuse targets [European Commission Issues Guidance on E.U. Packaging Rules Before 2026 Deadline].
*   **No Grace Period:** There is no grace period for placing non-compliant packaging on the market after August 12, 2026, raising fears that existing stock will become non-compliant waste [What the industry thinks as CEOs appeal to clarify and push back PPWR rules | Article | Packaging Europe].
*   **Cost Increases:** EPR fees will be eco-modulated based on recyclability. Industry experts warn that these compliance costs may accelerate the decline in bottled beer variety and increase consumer prices [EPR: what it means for the alcohol industry].

---

**Gaps and Limitations**
*   The findings do not specify the exact language requirements for mandatory information (other than allergens) for the Netherlands, Belgium, Spain, or Portugal, noting only that they must be easily understood.
*   The findings do not provide the exact date in 2026 when Poland's DRS becomes fully operational, only that it is expected in 2026.
*   While the findings note that health certificates are "normally not required" for beer, they do not definitively state the exact customs clearance documentation required for glass beer bottles beyond standard excise/VAT and labeling checks.
