## Query sent

> Identify the most significant supply-chain disruptions and risks over roughly the last 12 months affecting the global glass packaging and glass-bottle supply chain.
>
> Look across news, trade press and company announcements for events such as: energy-cost shocks affecting glass furnaces, plant or furnace closures, strikes, raw-material (soda ash, sand, cullet) shortages, freight and logistics disruruptions, and demand shifts.
>
> For each event, return:
> - What happened and when (with the date or period)
> - The region and any named companies affected
> - The impact on supply, capacity, lead times or price
> - A source URL
>
> Order the findings from most to least material, and note any recurring or structural risks the buyer should watch. Do not include events older than about 18 months unless they are still ongoing.

## Answer

**Executive Summary**

Over the past 18 months (early 2025 to mid-2026), the global glass packaging supply chain has been severely disrupted by compounding macroeconomic and geopolitical shocks. The most material event was the March 2026 West Asia crisis, which triggered a sudden suspension of Qatari liquefied natural gas (LNG) exports, causing European gas prices to spike up to 54% and forcing manufacturers to implement emergency energy surcharges of up to 2,000% on freight. Concurrently, major glass producers—including O-I Glass, Ardagh, and Verallia—executed aggressive capacity rationalizations, permanently closing or idling dozens of furnaces across the US and Europe to manage subdued demand and high operating costs. These structural reductions, combined with volatile US-China-Mexico tariff policies and ongoing Red Sea/Strait of Hormuz logistics bottlenecks, have significantly extended lead times and increased landed costs for beverage and pharmaceutical brands.

### 1. Geopolitical Energy Shocks and Surcharges (March 2026 Crisis)
**Materiality: High** | *Impact: Severe price inflation, localized production curtailments*

The glass industry, which requires 24/7 furnace operations and typically attributes 10% to 20% of total manufacturing costs to energy, faced a severe shock in early 2026 [O-I GLASS, INC._December 31, 2024](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/812074/000155837025000868/oi-20241231x10k.htm). 
*   **The Catalyst:** On March 2, 2026, QatarEnergy suspended production of LNG following drone and missile attacks on its Ras Laffan and Mesaieed plants during the US-Israeli war on Iran [Soaring gas prices and disrupted supply chains will ripple out to increase costs in every store and sector of the economy – Darden Report Online](https://news.darden.virginia.edu/2026/03/27/soaring-gas-prices-and-disrupted-supply-chains-will-ripple-out-to-increase-costs-in-every-store-and-sector-of-the-economy). QatarEnergy declared force majeure on gas exports on March 4, 2026, and later extended it through early September 2026 for Italy's Edison SpA [Exclusive: Qatar shuts gas liquefaction, will take weeks to restart, sources say | Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/qatarenergy-declares-force-majeure-lng-shipments-2026-03-04/) | [LNG market disruption: QatarEnergy extends force majeure with Edison](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/01/qatarenergy-extend-force-majeure-september-italys-edison-iran-war-.html).
*   **Price Impact:** European natural gas benchmark prices (TTF) surged by 35% to 54% immediately following the halt [QatarEnergy LNG Market Shift Reshapes Global Energy Security](https://discoveryalert.com.au/qatar-lng-market-2026-geopolitical-dynamics/) | [Gas Prices Surge as Qatar Shuts World’s Largest LNG Export Plant - Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-02/european-gas-rallies-more-than-30-as-qatar-halts-lng-production). Energy prices for glass manufacturing rose by nearly 67% at their peak during the crisis [Hindustan National Glass official says glass industry survived energy shock, but costs and gas supply remain concern- Moneycontrol.com](https://www.moneycontrol.com/news/business/companies/hindustan-national-glass-official-says-glass-industry-survived-energy-shock-but-costs-and-gas-supply-remain-concern-13898914.html).
*   **Surcharges:** Manufacturers rapidly passed costs down the supply chain. In the UK, energy surcharges on single lorry loads of glass rose from £350 to £6,500 (a reported 2,000% increase) in one week in March 2026 [Glass energy surcharges – ‘the cost must go down the supply chain’ | The Glazine](https://www.the-glazine.com/?p=5570) | [2,000 Percent Energy Surcharge Increase Rocks Glass Industry - Window News](https://www.windownews.co.uk/2000-percent-energy-surcharge-increase-rocks-glass-industry/). In the US, Hartung Glass Industries raised its energy surcharge to 16% for West Coast/Mountain West customers in January 2026, and EP Minerals added a $31.50 per short ton surcharge in March 2026 [‘Unprecedented’ Increase in Energy Surcharge Forces Hartung Glass to Adjust Rates](https://www.usglassmag.com/unprecedented-increase-in-energy-surcharge-forces-hartung-glass-to-adjust-rates/) | [Energy Surcharge Policy | EP Minerals](https://epminerals.com/about-us/customer-center/energy-surcharge-policy). Ecolab also announced a global 10-14% energy surcharge effective April 1, 2026 [Ecolab Announces Global 10-14% Energy Surcharge | Ecolab](https://www.ecolab.com/news/2026/03/ecolab-announces-global-10-14-energy-surcharge).
*   **Supply Impact:** Indian glass factories were forced to pare output in April 2026 due to energy shortages, impacting beverage and medical vial supplies [Gas Crunch Guts India Glassmakers Tripping Milk-to-Malt Supply Chains - Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-10/bottles-flasks-grow-scarce-in-india-as-gas-crunch-hits-glassmakers).

### 2. Structural Capacity Reductions and Plant Closures
**Materiality: High** | *Impact: Permanent reduction in global glass packaging capacity*

Faced with subdued demand, high debt, and elevated operating costs, major manufacturers executed aggressive network optimization programs throughout 2025 and 2026. 
*   **O-I Glass:** Realized over $300 million in benefits in 2025 from its 'Fit to Win' program, which involved idling or closing eight furnaces and eliminating approximately 1,500 jobs [O-I GLASS, INC._December 31, 2024](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/812074/000155837025000868/oi-20241231x10k.htm) | [O-I Glass reorienting portfolio to boost non-alcoholic beverages | Packaging Dive](https://www.packagingdive.com/news/oi-glass-q4-2025-packaging-earnings/811848/). Specific closures included its Portland, Oregon plant (August 2025), four Americas furnaces (2025), and halting furnaces in Vergèze and Vayres, France (late 2025/2026) [Major glass end user shuts down Oregon bottling plant - Resource Recycling](https://resource-recycling.com/recycling/2025/08/05/major-glass-end-user-shuts-down-oregon-bottling-plant/) | [O-I Glass to close four furnaces and a plant in Americas](https://www.glass-international.com/news/o-i-glass-to-close-four-furnaces-and-a-plant-in-americas) | [O-I plots short-term Vayres glass closure](https://www.glass-international.com/news/o-i-plots-short-term-vayres-glass-closure).
*   **Ardagh Group:** Facing a $700 million debt obligation in 2025 and a "maturity wall" in 2026, Ardagh closed its Dolton, Illinois plant (February 2025, 316 employees) and its Seattle, Washington facility (January 2026, 245 employees) [Ardagh: Through the Looking Glass - CreditSights](https://know.creditsights.com/insights/ardagh-through-the-looking-glass/) | [Ardagh Glass closing Illinois plant, affecting 316 employees | Packaging Dive](https://www.packagingdive.com/news/ardagh-glass-plant-closure-layoffs-dolton-illinois/739414/) | [Glass, EPS manufacturing hit in November layoff announcements | Packaging Dive](https://www.packagingdive.com/news/november-packaging-manufacturing-closure-layoff-announcements-anchor-ardagh-orora/733976/).
*   **Anchor Hocking:** Closed its 132-year-old Charleroi, Pennsylvania plant, resulting in over 300 layoffs. *(Note: Sources conflict on the exact date, with one citing April 2025 and another citing January 2026)* [Anchor Hocking - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anchor_Hocking) | [Pennsylvania glass plant closure sparks outrage | Manufacturing Dive](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/anchor-hocking-charleroi-pennsylvania-glass-pyrex-plant-closure-senators/728703/).
*   **Verallia:** Announced a business review in February 2026 that may result in up to 360 job cuts and potential closures at sites in Essen (Germany), Châteaubernard (France), and Knottingley (UK) [Verallia may cut up to 360 jobs as European glass bottle demand falls | Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/verallia-may-cut-up-360-jobs-european-glass-bottle-demand-falls-2026-02-17/) | [Verallia to cut up to 360 jobs](https://www.glass-international.com/news/verallia-to-cut-up-360-jobs).

### 3. Trade Policy Volatility and Tariffs
**Materiality: Moderate to High** | *Impact: Disrupted cross-border supply chains, increased landed costs*

*   **US Tariffs:** A baseline 10% tariff on imported goods, including glass packaging, took effect on April 5, 2025, followed by higher reciprocal tariffs on April 9, 2025. The total tariff rate on Chinese glass imports reached 54% to 55%, increasing the estimated landed cost of a standard wine bottle from $1.00 to $2.25 [2025 Tariff Updates: Impact on Packaging Supply Chains](https://evergreenresources.com/2025-packaging-industry-tariffs/) | [US Imposes 34% Tariffs On Chinese Imports - Industry News - News - Migo Glass](https://www.migoglass.com/news/us-imposes-34-tariffs-on-chinese-imports-84978513.html) | [How Trump's 104% Tariff Affects the Glass Bottle Industry?](https://www.crystalglassbottle.com/how-trumps-104-tariff-affects-the-glass-bottle-industry/). 
*   **Retaliation & Reversal:** Canada imposed 25% counter-tariffs on US goods, including float glass, starting March 4, 2025, causing US wine and spirits exports to Canada to plummet by up to 91% [List of products from the United States subject to 25 per cent tariffs effective March 4, 2025](https://www.canada.ca/en/department-finance/news/2025/03/list-of-products-from-the-united-states-subject-to-25-per-cent-tariffs-effective-march-4-2025.html) | [No U.S. Whiskey or Wine? Canada’s Doing Just Fine | Wine Enthusiast](https://www.wineenthusiast.com/culture/industry-news/canadian-whisky-wine-us-tariffs/). In early 2026, the US Supreme Court ruled that the President lacked authority for the broad reciprocal tariffs, leading to duty refunds [Mexico Tariffs 2026: What Manufacturers Actually Need to Know](https://insights.tetakawi.com/mexico-tariffs-2026-what-manufacturers-actually-need-to-know). However, Mexico implemented its own tariff reform on January 1, 2026, raising duties on glass bottles from non-FTA countries to as high as 35% [Mexico Raises Tariffs on Imports from China](https://www.allynintl.com/en/news-publications/entry/mexico-raises-tariffs-on-imports-from-china).

### 4. Freight Disruptions and the "Shortage" Contradiction
**Materiality: Moderate** | *Impact: Extended lead times, increased freight rates*

*   **Logistics Bottlenecks:** The 2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis and Red Sea rerouting caused surging freight rates and container shortages. Freight prices for transporting a 40-foot container (FEU) from Asia to the USA held around $5,300 in 2026 [2026 Container Shipping Outlook: Rising Costs, Delays & Solutions](https://www.foursonslogistics.com/blog/2026-container-shipping-costs-outlook/) | [What Awaits Us in 2026 in the Field of Shipping Containers - HZ CONTAINERS.com](https://hz-containers.com/en/news/what-awaits-us-in-2026-in-the-field-of-shipping-containers/). 
*   **Contested Shortage Narrative:** There is a stark contradiction in the market regarding supply availability. B2B suppliers claim standard delivery windows have stretched to several months in 2026, advising brands to maintain 90-day buffers *(Note: Claim sourced from low-confidence B2B marketing material)* [2026 Glass Bottle Supply News Shortages Trends and Solutions - Reihey Glass](https://reihey.com/glass-bottle-supply-news/). Conversely, the Glass Packaging Institute (GPI) explicitly characterized the "glass shortage" as a "false narrative" in late 2025, noting that 12 oz glass container imports were actually up 12% year-to-date [GPI refutes glass shortage claims](https://www.glass-international.com/news/gpi-refutes-glass-shortage-claims) | [Glass Packaging Institute (GPI) Industry Update](https://www.gpi.org/sites/default/files/content-files/West%20Coast%20Protective%20League%20Meeting,%20November%208th,%202025%20-%20GPI%20Deck-2-compressed.pdf). ### Analysis and Risk Assessment

Buyers of glass packaging face three structural risks heading into the second half of 2026:
1.  **Extreme Energy Vulnerability:** The industry's inability to pause furnace operations makes it uniquely susceptible to geopolitical energy shocks. The March 2026 QatarEnergy LNG suspension proved that regional conflicts immediately translate into massive, unavoidable freight and energy surcharges for buyers.
2.  **Consolidated Pricing Power & Antitrust Scrutiny:** The French Competition Authority raided Verallia and Saverglass in March 2025 on suspicion of illegal cartel activity and price coordination [Verallia, Saverglass part of anticompetition investigation in France | Packaging Dive](https://www.packagingdive.com/news/france-glass-raids-verallia-orora-saverglass-antitrust-investigation/743941/). While the Italian Competition Authority cleared manufacturers in January 2026, the UK Trade Remedies Authority launched new investigations into Chinese and Turkish imports in March 2026 [Verallia Acknowledges the Italian Competition Authority’s Decision Confirming Full Compliance With Competition Laws](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/verallia-acknowledges-italian-competition-authority-170000990.html) | [March in review: Iran war disrupts supply chains, regulation updates & Aicomp Summit 2026](https://www.packaginginsights.com/news/march-2026-packaging-update.html). This regulatory environment, paired with massive capacity closures by O-I and Ardagh, leaves buyers with fewer supplier alternatives and high exposure to coordinated price floors.
3.  **Permanent Capacity Loss:** The closures of 2025-2026 are largely permanent structural shifts rather than temporary idlings. Ardagh expects 2026 to be a "transition year" with volume declines before returning to growth in 2027 [Ardagh saw boost for cans, decline for glass in 2025 | Packaging Dive](https://www.packagingdive.com/news/ardagh-packaging-earnings-q4-full-year-2025-results-metal-glass/813311/).

### Gaps and Limitations
*   **Surcharge Specificity:** While major logistics providers and flat glass suppliers confirmed exact energy surcharge percentages, the findings lack explicit confirmation of the exact surcharge percentages applied *directly* to finished glass bottle invoices by the major packaging manufacturers (O-I, Ardagh, Verallia) following the March 2026 crisis.
*   **Date Discrepancies:** Sources conflict on the exact closure date of Anchor Hocking's Charleroi, PA plant (April 2025 vs. January 2026). 
*   **Shortage Severity:** The severity of the 2026 glass bottle "shortage" is highly contested; claims of severe bottlenecks rely heavily on low-confidence B2B marketing sources, which conflict with official statements from the Glass Packaging Institute.
