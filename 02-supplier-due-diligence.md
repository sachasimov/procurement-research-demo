## Query sent

> Produce a supplier due-diligence risk profile on the packaging manufacturer "Crown Holdings, Inc." (the beverage and food can maker).
>
> Cover, using public and reputable sources:
> - Corporate identity and ownership: legal entity, HQ, stock listing/ticker, ultimate parent, key subsidiaries
> - Financial health signals: latest reported revenue, profitability, debt or credit-rating commentary
> - Legal and regulatory exposure: notable litigation, regulatory actions, fines or settlements in the last 5 years
> - Sanctions or export-control exposure, if any
> - Product recalls or quality issues
> - ESG, environmental and labour controversies
>
> Return a short executive risk summary (low/medium/high with reasoning), then the detailed findings by category with dates. Cite the source URL for every material claim and explicitly flag gaps where evidence was not found.

## Answer

## Executive Summary

**Risk Rating: Medium**
Crown Holdings, Inc. is a financially stable, publicly traded global packaging manufacturer with strong reported revenues ($12.36 billion in 2025) and stable credit ratings. However, the company presents a medium supplier risk profile due to a recent history of antitrust penalties (a €31.5 million EU cartel settlement in 2022), ongoing U.S. customs litigation involving up to $18.1 million in potential fines, a $1.9 million environmental penalty in Washington state (2021–2023), and significant legacy asbestos liabilities. Additionally, there is conflicting open-source reporting regarding the extent of the company's continued operational footprint in Russia.

### Legal and Regulatory Exposure
*   **Antitrust Fines (EU):** On July 12, 2022, the European Commission fined Crown Holdings and Silgan a total of €31.5 million for participating in a metal packaging cartel in Germany between 2011 and 2014 [Violation Tracker - Good Jobs First](https://violationtracker.goodjobsfirst.org/violation-tracker/wv-crown-cork-and-seal-company-inc), [General Court Cans Crown and Silgan Appeals Against The Commission’s Metal Packaging Cartel Decision | Cleary Antitrust Watch](https://www.clearyantitrustwatch.com/2024/10/general-court-cans-crown-and-silgan-appeals-against-the-commissions-metal-packaging-cartel-decision/). Crown opted for a settlement procedure, admitting involvement in exchange for a 10% fine reduction [Antitrust: Commission fines the metal packaging producers Crown and Silgan €31.5 million in cartel settlement](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_22_4483). The EU General Court dismissed Crown's subsequent appeal on October 2, 2024 [General Court Cans Crown and Silgan Appeals Against The Commission’s Metal Packaging Cartel Decision | Cleary Antitrust Watch](https://www.clearyantitrustwatch.com/2024/10/general-court-cans-crown-and-silgan-appeals-against-the-commissions-metal-packaging-cartel-decision/).
*   **DOJ Customs Lawsuit:** In July 2021, the U.S. Department of Justice filed a lawsuit (Case 1:21-cv-00361) in the U.S. Court of International Trade against Crown Cork & Seal subsidiaries, seeking up to $18.1 million in penalties [Crown Holdings Faces $18 Million Lawsuit Over Metal Lid Imports](https://news.bloomberglaw.com/international-trade/crown-holdings-faces-18-million-lawsuit-over-metal-lid-imports). The DOJ alleged the company fraudulently misclassified 543 entries of metal lid imports between 2004 and 2009 to avoid over $1.1 million in duties [IN THE UNITED STATES COURT OF INTERNATIONAL TRADE BEFORE: THE UNITED STATES, )](https://millerco.com/sites/default/files/2021-08/US%20v.%20Crown%20Cork%20Seal%20CIT%20Complaint%20(00222295xB740D).PDF). The court issued an opinion in 2023, though the final settlement status is unconfirmed [United States v. Crown Cork & Seal USA, Inc., No. 21-00361 (Ct. Int'l Trade 2023) :: Justia](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/cit/21-00361/21-00361-2023-02-27.html).
*   **SEC Settlement:** In July 2022, Crown Holdings reached a settlement with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) relating to an investigation, though specific allegations were not disclosed [UNITED STATES SECURITIES AND EXCHANGE COMMISSION WASHINGTON, DC 20549 FORM 10-Q](https://crowncork.gcs-web.com/static-files/5c34d37b-9bcd-4a7b-b5bc-31c628e54158).

### Sanctions and Export-Control Exposure
*   **Sanctions:** There is no evidence in the provided findings that Crown Holdings, Inc. or its subsidiaries are currently designated under OFAC sanctions. *(Note: OFAC penalties against "King Holdings LLC", "Toll Holdings", and "Crown Holdings LLC" involve entirely distinct entities [OFAC Imposes $4.7 Million Penalty on Real Estate Investor for Dealing in Blocked Russian’s Property | U.S. Department of the Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0323), [Crown Holdings - Crown Holdings](https://crown.holdings/)).*
*   **Russia Operations:** Crown Holdings' current operational status in Russia is contested. The company historically operated a packaging line in Krasnodar Krai, Russia, in partnership with Bonduelle [Bonduelle and Crown Partner In Russia | Crown](https://www.crowncork.com/news/bonduelle-and-crown-partner-russia). Crown divested its European Tinplate business (Eviosys) between 2021 and December 2024 [Crown Holdings, Inc. to Receive Proceeds From Closing of Eviosys Sale to Sonoco Products Company | Crown](https://www.crowncork.com/news/crown-holdings-inc-receive-proceeds-closing-eviosys-sale-sonoco-products-company). The company's 2024 Annual Report does not list any facilities in Russia under its European Beverage segment [A N N U A L R E P O R T 2 0 2 4 BUILT TO PERFORM](https://www.crowncork.com/sites/default/files/2025-03/2024_Annual_Report-Crown_Holdings_Inc.pdf). However, the monitoring project 'LeaveRussia' categorized Crown Holdings as "Staying" and "Doing Business in Russia as Usual" as of June 2022 [LeaveRussia: Crown Holdings is Doing Business in Russia as Usual](https://leave-russia.org/crown-holdings). 

### Product Recalls and Quality Issues
*   **Asbestos Product Liability:** Crown Holdings faces significant legacy product liability litigation stemming from its 1963 acquisition of Mundet Cork, an asbestos insulation manufacturer [Crown Cork & Seal Co. | Asbestos Products & Lawsuits](https://www.mesothelioma.com/asbestos-exposure/companies/crown-cork-seal-company/). As of 2022, the company estimated its current and future asbestos-related liabilities at approximately $220 million [Crown Cork & Seal Co. | Asbestos Products & Lawsuits](https://www.mesothelioma.com/asbestos-exposure/companies/crown-cork-seal-company/). Notable past losses include a 2012 Louisiana jury verdict of $12 million (Crown found 33% responsible) and a $5.1 million award to the family of William Saller [Crown Cork and Seal Lawsuits and Former Insurance Fund](https://www.asbestos.com/companies/crown-cork-and-seal/), [Crown Cork & Seal | Asbestos Exposure and Lawsuits](https://www.mesotheliomalawyercenter.org/asbestos/manufacturers/crown-cork-seal/).

### ESG, Environmental, and Labor Controversies
*   **Environmental Fines:** Crown Cork & Seal Company agreed to pay $1,945,305 to the Olympic Region Clean Air Agency (ORCAA) to settle nine air quality violations occurring between 2021 and 2023 at its Olympia, Washington facility [Crown Cork & Seal agrees to fines for repeated air quality violations | The JOLT News Organization](https://www.thejoltnews.com/stories/crown-cork-seal-agrees-to-fines-for-repeated-air-quality-violations,13865). Violations included bypassing emission control equipment, exceeding permissible formaldehyde limits, and beginning construction without permits [Crown Cork & Seal agrees to fines for repeated air quality violations | The JOLT News Organization](https://www.thejoltnews.com/stories/crown-cork-seal-agrees-to-fines-for-repeated-air-quality-violations,13865). Violation Tracker reports the company has accumulated $10,706,380 in total penalties from 69 records since 2000 [crown-holdings-inc | Violation Tracker](https://violationtracker.goodjobsfirst.org/parent/crown-holdings-inc).
*   **Labor Disputes:** The company has a history of contentious labor relations. In September 2013, 120 United Steelworkers members at a Toronto factory initiated a strike lasting at least 17 months over demanded 42% pay cuts for new employees and reduced pensions, which sparked a consumer boycott in Canada [Crown boycott reaches US settlement](https://www.beveragedaily.com/Article/2015/04/20/Crown-boycott-reaches-US-settlement/), [Global fight for workers’ rights continues at Crown Holdings](https://www.industriall-union.org/global-fight-for-workers-rights-continues-at-crown-holdings). The company also faced allegations in Turkey of dismissing union activists and refusing to negotiate with officially recognized unions [Global fight for workers’ rights continues at Crown Holdings](https://www.industriall-union.org/global-fight-for-workers-rights-continues-at-crown-holdings).
